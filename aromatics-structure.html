Benzene

Benzene is represented as a six carbon ring that has three double bonds in its cyclic structure. These three pi bonds are able to move from each carbon bond to the next freely, resulting in delocalized electrons, these delocalized electrons result in the compound being incredibly stable. This means that while the compound has three pi bonds, it is considered/acts like its saturated.
